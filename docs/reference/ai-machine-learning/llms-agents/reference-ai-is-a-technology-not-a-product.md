# AI is a technology not a product

## Summary
AI is a pervasive underlying technology, not a standalone product category. Apple does not need to launch a "killer AI product" to remain competitive; instead, AI will integrate seamlessly across its existing hardware ecosystem, mirroring the industry-wide adoption of wireless networking.

## Key Points
- **Apple's Product Philosophy**: Apple ships experiences, not raw technology. The iPod wasn't marketed as an MP3 player — it was sold as a music experience.
- **AI Won't Obviate the iPhone Ecosystem**: AI will change the iPhone ecosystem, but not replace it. The "cloud computing" hype analogy applies — vague and overblown.
- **"Always-On AI Agent" Hype is Implausible**: Practical questions remain: What hardware captures voice commands? What displays feedback? How are errors handled?
- **The Phone Remains Primary**: By 2030, the phone will still be the primary device for interacting with AI, whether via voice or touch.
- **Wireless Networking Analogy**: AI mirrors Wi-Fi/Bluetooth adoption — Apple never needed a "killer wireless product"; wireless became a foundational layer across all devices. AI will follow the same path.

## Key Quote
> "AI, on the other hand, is pervasive. It can't be ignored. But it's just technology. Wireless networking is pervasive too. But Apple doesn't have 'a killer wireless networking product.' Wireless networking simply pervades everything Apple makes... That's more what AI is going to be like. There's not going to be one 'killer AI device'. Everything is going to be an AI device, to some extent."
> — John Gruber, Daring Fireball
