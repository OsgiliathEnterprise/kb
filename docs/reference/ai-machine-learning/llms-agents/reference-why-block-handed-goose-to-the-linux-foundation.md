# Why Block handed Goose to the Linux Foundation

## Summary
Block open-sourced **Goose**, its internal AI coding agent deployed to 12,000 employees, and transferred it to the newly formed **Agentic AI Foundation (AAIF)** hosted under the Linux Foundation. This resolves governance transparency issues and removes enterprise adoption barriers.

## Key Points
- **Governance Transparency**: Rapid public adoption exposed lack of clear, open governance structures for Goose
- **Enterprise Adoption Friction**: Block retained trademark ownership, creating legal/compliance concerns for enterprise users
- **Neutral Stewardship**: Linux Foundation ensures long-term sustainability, community-driven development, and vendor neutrality

## The Agentic AI Foundation (AAIF)
- **Leadership**: Co-founded and led by **Manik Surtani** (former head of open-source at Block, now AAIF CTO)
- **Hosting**: Operates as arm of the Linux Foundation
- **Initial Core Projects**: Goose (coding agent), MCP (Model Context Protocol), Agents.MD
- **Stakeholder Buy-in**: Strong participation from AWS, Anthropic, and major tech corporations

## Strategic Implications
- **Enterprise Readiness**: Neutral foundation governance removes trademark/legal friction for corporate deployment
- **Standardization Push**: Co-locating Goose, MCP, and Agents.MD accelerates interoperability standards for AI agents
- **Linux Foundation Expansion**: Positions LF as central hub for agentic AI infrastructure (mirroring its cloud-native role with Kubernetes, Prometheus)
- **Community-Driven Roadmap**: Development shifts from single-company to multi-stakeholder governance
